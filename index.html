Why we're building this

Most shops still run on memory.

Spreadsheets track the order and email tracks the proof, but nobody tracks the phone call where a family says yes. We're building MonuManage to fix that.

01

Approvals happen by phone

A family says they never approved that change to the base. Nobody wrote it down, so nobody can prove otherwise.

02

Files live in six places

The proof is in an email thread, the signed contract is in a drawer, and the site photos are on somebody's phone.

03

One person holds the rules

Hillside Cemetery requires flush markers under 24 inches. That fact lives in one employee's head, and she's out sick this week.

Order tracking

Family portal

Families will approve a design without a phone call.

Send a secure link. No account, no password required on their end. The family opens it, sees the current proof and the order status, and clicks Approve or Request Changes.

Approval carries a timestamp and locks that exact proof version, so there's a record if the question ever comes up again. A revision request opens a comment thread your staff can see directly on the order.

The preview alongside is interactive, and it's the exact flow we're building. Try it.

Security, from day one

Your data, and your families' data, kept separate by company.

These are build requirements, in place before the first customer signs in.

  • Every query filtered by company, enforced on the server, never trusted from the browser.
  • Data encrypted in transit and at rest, on every plan.
  • Multi-factor authentication required for owner and admin accounts.
  • Backups encrypted and restore-tested on a fixed schedule.
